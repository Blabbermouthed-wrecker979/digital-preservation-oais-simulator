    Recommended formats by role — Preservation vs. Access
    MASTER
    TIFF
    ISO 12234 · FADGI · Metamorfoze
    Lossless raster image. Master format for digitisation projects. Preserves all pixel data without destructive compression.
    MASTER · ACCESS
    PDF/A
    ISO 19005-1/2/3
    Self-contained, software-independent. International preservation standard for electronic records with legal and probative value.
    PRESERVATION
    PDF/A-3
    ISO 19005-3
    Allows embedding native attachments inside PDF/A. Ideal for mixed records packages with XML, structured data, or hybrid files.
    ACCESS
    JPEG2000
    ISO/IEC 15444
    Configurable lossy or lossless compression. Access derivatives for high-resolution image collections (libraries, archives, museums).
    METADATA
    XML / PREMIS
    ISO 23081 · PREMIS 3.0
    Structured preservation metadata. Documents events, agents, rights and relationships of the digital object across its lifecycle.
    AVOID
    JPEG / DOC / XLS
    No preservation standard
    Proprietary or lossy formats. Not suitable as preservation masters. Require immediate migration to open, standards-based formats.

    Digital Migration Strategies — ISO 14721:2025 Section 5
    Select a strategy to explore its description, risk level and conditions of application. The wrong choice can compromise the authenticity of digital objects — a critical concern for legal and archival repositories.
    🔁 Refreshment
    Replaces a storage media instance without altering any bits. The digital object remains bit-for-bit identical. Lowest risk migration type.
    LOW RISK
    📋 Replication
    Exact copy of the AIP without any alteration. Implements the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 different media types, 1 offsite location.
    LOW RISK
    📦 Repackaging
    Alters the Packaging Information of the AIP without modifying the Content Information. Requires PREMIS event documentation.
    MEDIUM RISK
    ⚗ Transformation
    Alters the Content Information or the PDI. Requires authenticity validation, exhaustive documentation and institutional approval.

    Integrity Verification — Checksums & PREMIS
    Fixity is one of the 5 mandatory PDI categories (ISO 14721:2025). Without periodic checksum verification, no repository can guarantee the authenticity of its objects. This is a core requirement for ISO 16363 certification.

    The 3-2-1 backup rule
    3 — Three copies of the object
    1 production copy + 2 backup copies. No copy is "the original" — all are equal replicas.
    2 — Two different media types
    Hard disk + magnetic tape, or on-premises server + cloud. Different manufacturers and technologies.
    1 — One copy offsite
    Different geographic location. Protects against physical disasters: fire, flood, earthquake, theft.
    Supported integrity algorithms
    AlgorithmLengthRecommended use
    SHA-256256 bitsCurrent standard for digital preservation. Recommended by PREMIS, OAIS, and Library of Congress.
    SHA-512512 bitsHigher security for critical collections. Used in ISO 16363-certified repositories worldwide.
    MD5128 bitsLegacy — acceptable for error detection only. Not suitable for security. Known collision vulnerabilities.
    CRC-3232 bitsTransmission error detection only. Insufficient for long-term preservation assurance.
